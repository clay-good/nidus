# Code of Conduct

Nidus exists to reduce maternal and fetal suffering by making it easier
for researchers to ask sharper questions. The community around the
project should reflect that purpose. This Code of Conduct describes the
behaviour expected of everyone who participates in the project, in any
capacity, and the process by which violations are addressed.

## Our standards

Participants in this project are expected to:

- **Be scientifically honest.** Do not overstate the certainty of a
  claim, do not represent a parameter as better-supported than its
  source literature shows, and do not contribute changes that would
  obscure rather than surface uncertainty. The simulator's value
  depends on its honesty; protecting that honesty is everyone's job.
- **Engage respectfully.** Disagreements about scientific content,
  modelling choices, or implementation details are welcome and
  expected. Personal attacks, harassment, demeaning language, and
  bad-faith argument are not. The standard is the same in pull request
  comments, issue threads, and any other project venue.
- **Credit sources.** Cite the literature you draw on, name the people
  whose ideas shaped a contribution, and acknowledge co-authors of work
  you build on.
- **Assume good faith.** A new contributor who misuses the tier system,
  cites a source incorrectly, or proposes an unconventional approach is
  almost always doing so honestly. Help them get to a correct
  contribution rather than treating the mistake as a character flaw.

## Unacceptable behaviour

The following are not tolerated in any project venue:

- Harassment, threats, or intimidation, public or private.
- Discriminatory language or behaviour on any basis, including but not
  limited to gender, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, religion,
  national origin, disability, or age.
- Publishing private information about another participant without
  their explicit permission.
- Deliberately misrepresenting the scientific content of the
  simulator, including pressuring reviewers to merge changes that
  inflate confidence tiers, omit citations, or hide validation
  failures.
- Sustained disruption of project discussions or processes.

## Reporting

Reports of unacceptable behaviour can be sent to the project
maintainers at the contact address listed in the repository's
maintainer file (forthcoming). Reports are handled confidentially.
Reporters will not be penalised for raising concerns in good faith
even if the report is not ultimately found to warrant action.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of
Conduct and will respond to reports in a manner appropriate to the
circumstances. Responses may include private discussion, public
correction, temporary or permanent removal of contribution
privileges, or escalation to relevant authorities in severe cases.

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the principles of the Contributor
Covenant and is tailored to the scientific commitments of Nidus.
